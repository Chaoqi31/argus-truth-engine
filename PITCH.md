# Argus — 200-word intro

> Submission blurb for the UCWS Singapore × MiroMind Deep Research track.
> Kept under 200 words; uses only claims the system actually delivers.

---

**Argus — the audit layer for AI-generated content.**

Compliance officers, lawyers, and research analysts increasingly have to act on
documents they didn't write: vendor risk memos, AI-drafted briefs, third-party
research notes. They must trust the AI before they act — but they have no way to
check it.

Argus audits any AI-generated artifact. Upload a PDF or paste text; it extracts
every factual claim and independently verifies each one through autonomous deep
research across academic registries, public filings, and primary data sources.
For every claim it returns a verdict, the **reasoning chain** that produced it,
and a **clickable evidence trail** — catching fabricated citations, inaccurate
numbers, misrepresented sources, outdated data, and internal contradictions. For
each, it explains *why* it's wrong and *what the correct answer is*.

Unlike Patronus, Galileo, or Vectara — which sell inline scoring to the
*producers* of AI — Argus serves the *consumer*: the person who must trust AI
output they didn't create. Every verdict is transparent, traceable, and
exportable as a filed audit report.
